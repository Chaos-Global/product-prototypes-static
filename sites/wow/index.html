Raids are multi-boss instances designed for coordinated groups. They usually demand mechanic awareness, role discipline, and patience while learning.

10–30
Typical group size depending on mode and expansion design.

Understand the raid roles

If you know what every role is supposed to do, you make better decisions even when playing only one of them.

🛡 Tank

Control the fight

Tanks position bosses, manage threat, swap when mechanics require it, and use defensives for heavy damage windows.

  • Face bosses away when needed.
  • Move enemies cleanly and predictably.
  • Use mitigation before damage spikes, not after.
  • Coordinate taunt swaps with the other tank.
✚ Healer

Stabilize the group

Healers prepare for incoming raid damage, dispel important effects, and keep cooldowns ready for dangerous phases.

  • Track major damage timers.
  • Save mana when the fight allows it.
  • Coordinate healing cooldowns.
  • Dispel only when it is safe and assigned.
⚔ DPS

Handle mechanics and damage

Good DPS players do strong damage without ignoring movement, interrupts, target swaps, or survival tools.

  • Do mechanics first, parse later.
  • Use personal defensives often.
  • Swap to priority targets quickly.
  • Interrupt, stun, or soak when assigned.

How to do raids in World of Warcraft

This is the practical flow most players follow when learning a raid from scratch or joining a new group.

01

Choose your difficulty and group

Start with Raid Finder or Normal if you are new. Heroic and Mythic require stronger execution, tighter preparation, and more coordination.

02

Learn the boss mechanics

Do not try to memorize everything. Focus on your personal responsibilities: where to stand, what to interrupt, what to soak, and when to move.

03

Set up your UI

Track boss timers, debuffs, and important cooldowns. A clean UI helps you react faster than a cluttered one.

04

Listen to assignments

Raid leaders often assign interrupts, dispels, positioning groups, or defensive rotations. Missing an assignment can wipe the raid.

05

Survive first, optimize later

Dead players do zero damage and zero healing. Learn the dance of the fight before worrying about perfect output.

06

Review mistakes after pulls

Progress comes from identifying what killed the group: missed interrupts, bad positioning, late cooldowns, or failed mechanics.

Golden rule: In raids, mechanics are part of your rotation. Moving correctly, soaking correctly, and using defensives on time are not optional extras.

Common boss-mechanic mindset

Even though every raid tier is different, many encounters use familiar patterns. Learning these patterns helps you adapt faster.

Typical mechanics you will see

Spread

Move away from other players so splash damage or debuff explosions do not chain across the raid.

Stack

Group up tightly to split damage, receive healing, or bait mechanics into predictable locations.

Soak

Stand in a marker when assigned so a mechanic does not explode across the whole raid.

Interrupt or purge

Some casts must never complete. Know which targets are priority utility targets.

New raider survival advice

  • Watch your feet. Ground effects are raid classics for a reason.
  • Use defensives proactively when a timer or callout warns you.
  • Do not overlap the raid leader’s voice with panic chatter.
  • Ask questions between pulls, not during critical mechanics.
  • Accept wipes as part of learning rather than proof you are failing.
  • Keep improving one mechanic at a time.
